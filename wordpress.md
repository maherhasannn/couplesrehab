# Rehab Content Skill

## What this produces
One enterprise-grade article (4,000–8,000 words) that is the most complete answer online for its target keyword, plus all publishing assets (title, meta, schema, links, etc.). It must satisfy intent so fully that users don't bounce back to search.

## Before writing: lock the inputs
Confirm or ask for:
- **Primary keyword** (e.g. "couples rehab," "fentanyl detox")
- **Geo target** (city/state) if applicable
- **Facility specifics**: which levels of care offered, insurances accepted, same-day capability, phone number for CTAs
- **Internal pages available to link** (detox, couples, insurance, withdrawal, emergency, location pages)

If facility specifics are unknown, write with placeholders like `[PHONE]` and `[INSURANCE LIST]` rather than inventing facts.

## Reader state — write to this first
These searches happen in crisis. For every section, assume the reader is one of:
- A spouse/parent panicking about overdose, withdrawal, or a refusing loved one
- Someone in active withdrawal afraid of seizures/DTs/death
- A couple deciding whether to get treatment together
- Someone confused about whether insurance covers it
- Someone who needs help *today*

Lead with reassurance + a clear next step, then deliver depth. The emotional payload comes first; the SEO depth keeps them on the page.

## YMYL / E-E-A-T guardrails (non-negotiable)
This is medical content. Follow strictly:
- No promises of recovery, no cure language, no success-rate claims you can't source
- Medically cautious phrasing ("can be life-threatening," not "will kill you")
- Name real risks honestly (alcohol/benzo withdrawal can be fatal; recommend medical supervision)
- Direct readers to **call 911 / 988** for emergencies — say this explicitly where overdose or severe withdrawal is discussed
- Write as a knowledgeable treatment authority, not a salesperson

## Article structure

**1. Above-the-fold intro (150–250 words)**
Name the fear directly, give immediate reassurance, state that help/same-day admission exists, plant first CTA. No throat-clearing.

**2. Direct-answer block**
A 40–60 word plain-language answer to the literal query, formatted for AI Overviews and featured snippets. Put it high.

**3. Core depth sections** — cover what's relevant to the keyword:

*Addiction treatment:* detox → residential → PHP → IOP → MAT → therapy → couples track → dual diagnosis → relapse prevention → aftercare.

*Withdrawal & detox:* symptoms, why medical detox matters, substance-specific notes (fentanyl/opioid, alcohol, benzo, meth), timelines, danger signs (seizures, DTs, delirium), when it's an emergency.

*Couples rehab:* can couples attend together, couples detox, codependency, rebuilding trust, boundaries, joint relapse prevention, family reintegration.

*Insurance & payment:* PPO basics, verification process, in- vs out-of-network, major carriers (Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, United), same-day verification, private pay/financing.

*Emergency help:* same-day/24-hour admission, crisis stabilization, intervention basics, overdose warning signs, when to call 911, what intake looks like.

**4. Engagement elements** (use throughout, not all at once)
Comparison tables, symptom lists, treatment timelines, crisis checklists, "what happens next" step frameworks, scenario examples. These drive scroll depth and dwell time.

**5. FAQ section — 15–25 questions**
Pull from: detox, withdrawal, couples rehab, insurance, emergency admission, relapse, intervention, family support, same-day treatment. Answers concise (2–4 sentences), medically cautious, phrased the way people actually ask. Format for FAQ schema.

**6. Closing CTA**
Strong "don't wait" close with phone/admission prompt and crisis-line mention.

## Keyword strategy
Target the primary keyword plus natural variations: singular + plural, "near me," "emergency," "same day," "with insurance," and emotional long-tail phrasing ("my husband is addicted what do I do," "my wife refuses rehab," "is alcohol withdrawal dangerous"). Work them in naturally — no stuffing. Reinforce entities throughout: medical detox, substance use disorder, dual diagnosis, MAT, residential treatment, behavioral healthcare, crisis intervention, couples therapy.

## CTA placement
Place action prompts at: intro, after the first major depth section, mid-article, and close. Vary them — same-day admission, insurance verification, "call now," crisis-line. Keep them reassuring, never pushy.

## Internal linking
Link contextually with natural anchor text to: detox, couples rehab, insurance, withdrawal, mental health, emergency help, location, same-day, telehealth, and family support pages. Aim for relevance over volume.

## External linking
Cite authoritative sources to ground claims: SAMHSA, NIDA, NIH, CDC, NIAAA, DEA, Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, MedlinePlus, 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, relevant state behavioral-health departments. Link where they support a factual claim.

## Tone
Compassionate, authoritative, medically aware, calm. Avoid: SEO filler, generic AI phrasing, keyword stuffing, fearmongering, clinical coldness, unrealistic promises.
