# Rehab Content Skill (couplesrehab.com)

## Reference page
The canonical model for output is `/couples-detox-rhode-island/`. When in doubt about structure, depth, voice, or section order, match that page.

## What this produces
One deep-clinical article (geo and pillar pages: 4,000–6,000+ words; emergency-intent pages run shorter — see URL map) that is the most complete, most clinically credible answer online for its keyword, with 3 embedded CTA boxes, plus all publishing assets. It must read as written by someone who actually knows addiction medicine.

## The site & voice (read first — non-negotiable)
CouplesRehab.com presents as a **national addiction treatment placement and referral network**. Active, capable voice: "we," "our placement team," "we verify benefits," "we coordinate admission," "available 24/7."

**The only conversion goal: phone calls to 888-500-2110.** The Couples Assessment is secondary.

Two guardrails that make the active voice defensible (the RI page keeps both — so do we):
1. Always include the line that **Couples Rehab is a placement/referral network and NOT a treatment facility.** Put it in the opening and the editorial disclaimer.
2. **Never guarantee** coverage outcomes, in-network status, out-of-pocket figures, or a specific joint-placement configuration. Frame joint placement as "regularly possible, often clinically beneficial, never guaranteed ahead of time" and coverage as "verified before any commitment."

CTA verbs: "Call 888-500-2110," "call a care navigator," "begin the placement process," "verify your benefits," "Take the Couples Assessment." The team verifies benefits and coordinates admission — but does not itself provide treatment.

## Reader state — write to this first
Crisis searches: panicking spouse/parent, someone in active withdrawal, a couple deciding to get help together, insurance confusion, someone who needs help today. Open with reassurance + the call, then deliver clinical depth. Realistic clarity, not promotional language — the RI page explicitly avoids hype, and that restraint is part of why it reads as authoritative.

## Clinical depth requirement (this is the differentiator)
Generic "medically cautious" prose loses. Match the RI page's register:
- Name **assessment scales** where relevant (CIWA-Ar for alcohol, COWS for opioids).
- Give **withdrawal timelines by hour/day** per substance, with named phases (e.g. alcohol: early 6–24h, mid 24–72h, seizure window 12–48h, DTs 48–96h).
- Name **medication classes and specific agents** (benzodiazepines for alcohol detox; buprenorphine/methadone/naltrexone for opioids; clonidine/ondansetron/loperamide comfort meds; thiamine/folate supplementation; longer-acting benzo taper).
- Cite **standards of care** (e.g. ASAM endorsement of MAT initiation) without overclaiming.
- Cover substance classes separately: alcohol, opioids/fentanyl/heroin/Rx opioids, benzodiazepines, stimulants (meth/cocaine), polysubstance — each with its own symptom profile and danger level.
- Be honest about danger gradients: alcohol & benzo withdrawal carry seizure/DTs risk (medical detox not optional); opioid withdrawal is intensely uncomfortable but not directly life-threatening for stable adults; stimulant withdrawal is psychiatric (crash, depression, suicidality, possible psychosis) not pharmacologically driven.
- Localize fentanyl/overdose context to the region when relevant (tolerance reset + contaminated supply = elevated post-detox overdose risk).

## Geo localization (richer than a placeholder)
For geo pages: name 8–10 cities/areas in the state, each with a sentence of real local context (role as treatment hub, proximity to the main metro, cross-state options). Reference feasible cross-state placement and link to neighboring-state cluster pages. Note the metro that concentrates treatment infrastructure. Never "our [city] facility" — "providers/programs accessible from [city]," "facilities that admit couples in the [metro] area."

## FAQ
15–25 questions, 2–4 sentence answers, medically cautious, phrased as people actually search. Cover: can couples [X] together, what is [topic], is medical detox necessary for [substance], substance-specific danger, what happens during intake, how long does it last, what happens after, does insurance cover it, dual diagnosis, one partner unwilling, signs someone needs detox, is detox enough, what to bring, how to get help today. Format for FAQPage schema.

## Keyword strategy
Primary keyword from the map + natural variations (singular/plural, "near me," "emergency," "same day," "with insurance," emotional long-tail). No stuffing. Reinforce entities: medical detox, substance use disorder, dual diagnosis, MAT, residential treatment, withdrawal management, CIWA-Ar/COWS, behavioral health, crisis intervention, couples therapy, telehealth.

## CTA placement (text prompts, in addition to the 3 boxes)
Beyond the 3 styled boxes, weave call-forward language into the numbered "how to get help" steps and the closing crisis blockquote — every prompt leads to 888-500-2110. Reassuring, never pushy.

## Internal linking
Per the map: UP to parent pillar, SIDEWAYS to 2–3 cluster siblings + neighboring-geo pages, plus `/couples-assessment/` and `/crisis-support/`. Telehealth topics link `/telehealth/`. Dense but natural contextual anchors, as on the RI page.

## External linking
Named authorities only, where they support a claim: SAMHSA (findtreatment.samhsa.gov), NIDA, CDC overdose prevention, NIH, NIAAA, the relevant state health department, 988 Lifeline. ASAM may be referenced for standards of care.

## Tone
Compassionate, clinically authoritative, calm, realistic. Avoid: SEO filler, generic AI phrasing, keyword stuffing, fearmongering, unrealistic promises, provider-cosplay claims ("our facility admits"), and any coverage/placement guarantee.
